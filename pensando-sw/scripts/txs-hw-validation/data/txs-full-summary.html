txs SQ fast-disable — HW validation summary

Vulcano / hydra Meta-RoCE · feature branch pradeept/hydra-sq-txs-disable (d7960d67471, 1.130.2-a base) · consolidated across 3 campaigns · 2026-07-21

What the feature is

txs_cmd is a Vulcano-only SPR fast-path (__mtspr(__SPRID_TXS_CMD,…)) that stops the data SQ TX scheduler in one instruction when the SQ drains, so it stops emitting spurious "end-of-SQ" PHVs; a race-free sched_eval in S2 restarts it when new work arrives. It lets the SQ run auto-clear OFF (wanted for fairness at scale) without the spurious-PHV churn that auto-clear-off otherwise creates on drain.

The three configs (2 knobs, same tree)

AC-on
auto-clear ON, no txs
AC-off
auto-clear OFF, no txs — current shipping
TXS
auto-clear OFF + txs SQ fast-disable — the feature

Knobs: RDMA_USE_TXS_CMD (rdma.h) and cos3 tx_scheduler3.auto_clear (device-pf2 json). Primary metric phb_drops = spurious PHVs (== NPV−PSP). Image identity proven per-run by the 1-QP drain self-check ladder AC-on ≈75M < TXS ≈113M < AC-off ≈223M. Naming: these map to B1/B2/F in the detailed reports & raw archives — AC-on = B1, AC-off = B2, TXS = F.

Bottom line

PASS The feature works and carries no regression, at 400G and 800G, on IB microbenchmarks and on real RCCL collectives.

  • Benefit (drain): under a draining SQ, TXS cuts the shipping AC-off spurious end-of-SQ PHVs ~50% at low QP (tapering at high QP), at 400G and 800G.
  • No regression (saturated): zero BW / latency / packet-drop cost — SMC ~753 Gb/s, Kenya ~1.5 Tbps (800G line rate); RCCL busBw within <1% of shipping across all 6 collectives.
  • Nuance (RCCL A-B-C-A): a genuine but tiny throughput ordering AC-off ≥ TXS ≥ AC-on — AC-on is the slowest (up to ~1.2% on alltoall); TXS within ≤0.4% of AC-off.

2 · Mechanism & cost — asicmon scheduler events drain, RCN off

How each config trades scheduler activity for spurious PHVs. Doorbell Sched0 = SQ scheduler wake-ups; TXs0 Clear = scheduler-stop events. AC-off runs the scheduler lean but leaks spurious PHVs; AC-on suppresses them via constant scheduler churn; TXS removes ~half the spurious PHVs with a small, targeted amount of extra scheduler work. Values in millions (M); pair with the spurious-PHV counts in §1.
